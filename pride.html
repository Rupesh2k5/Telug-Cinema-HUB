Pioneers and Early Visionaries



Raghupathi Venkaiah Naidu

Often know as"Father of Telugu Cinema"

He was a filmmaker and exhibitor involved in producing short films and establishing South India's first permanent cinema halls in the early 1900s. He produced the first Telugu silent feature film, Bhishma Pratigna (1921).

  • He established the first permanent, Indian-owned cinema halls in Madras (now Chennai), including Gaiety Theatre (1912)
  • The Raghupathi Venkaiah Award, part of the Nandi Awards, is named in his honor to recognize lifetime achievements in Telugu cinema, cementing his legacy.
  • Produced films like Bhishma Pratigna, considered the first full-length Telugu feature film.

Hanumappa Muniappa Reddy (H.M. Reddy)

Director

He directed the first Indian multilingual sound film Kalidas (1931), shot in Telugu and Tamil.

  • He produced and directed the first full length Telugu sound film, Bhakta Prahlada in 1932.

C. Pullayya

Director

He is one of the earliest film personalities in Telugu cinema being associated with the industry right from the silent era.

  • He directed Sati Savitri (1933), which received international recognition at the 2nd Venice Film Festival, and Lava Kusa (1934), the industry's first major commercial success.
  • C. Pullayya was a pioneering Telugu film director known for landmark films like Lava Kusa (1934 & 1963), Sati Savitri (1933), Bala Nagamma (1942), Paramanandayya Sishyula Katha (1966).
  • He directed many mythological and historical films, starting with Sati Savitri, which won an honorary diploma at the Venice Film Festival.
  • His 1963 Lava Kusa won a National Film Award for Best Telugu Film.

L.V. Prasad

L.V. Prasad was a pioneer in Telugu cinema, contributing as an actor, director, and producer, notably appearing in the first Telugu talkie, Bhakta Prahlada (1932).

  • Made his directorial debut with the critically acclaimed Grihapravesham (1946) and directed many other significant Telugu films like Palnati Yuddham (1947) and Samsaram (1950).
  • First recipient of the Raghupathi Venkaiah Award (1980) for his contribution to Telugu cinema.
  • Received the Dadasaheb Phalke Award (1982) for his lifetime achievement in Indian cinema.

The Golden Age (1950s and 1960s) and Key Actors


This era was marked by high-quality productions and influential filmmakers.


Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (N.T.R)

N. T. Rama Rao (NTR) revolutionized the Telugu film industry by popularizing mythological roles (like Krishna and Rama) that made him a superstar, diversifying into social dramas, and becoming a prolific producer and director who won National Film Awards for his work.

  • He established his own production house, built a studio, and pioneered a new era of Telugu cinema with blockbusters.
  • He became famous for playing Hindu deities, particularly Lord Krishna and Lord Rama, portraying Krishna 17 times and becoming a household name.
  • He starred in over 300 films, including major hits in social dramas like Adavi Ramudu (1977) and Bobbili Puli (1982).
  • He was a key figure in bringing new themes and large-scale productions to Telugu cinema
  • His cinematic stardom translated directly into political power when he founded the Telugu Desam Party (TDP) in 1982, becoming Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh in 1983 and using his platform to promote Telugu identity and welfare.
  • He set a precedent for actors transitioning into politics and changed the landscape of both Telugu cinema and politics.
  • He Always Remains as "Ramayya" in every telugu people Heart
  • Telugu People named him as "Viswa Vikhyatha Nata Sarvabhouma"

Akkineni Nageswara Rao (ANR)

ANR brought emotional depth and realism to Telugu cinema. He played a key role in shifting the industry base to Hyderabad and represented Telugu cinema with dignity across India.

  • Akkineni Nageswara Rao (ANR) was a legendary actor with a career spanning over seven decades, starring in 256 films, known for romantic leads in movies like Devadasu
  • His contributions include moving the Telugu film industry base from Madras to Hyderabad, establishing Annapurna Studios, and being a significant entrepreneur.
  • Earned Awards like the Padma Vibhushan and the Dadasaheb Phalke Award.
  • Over his seven decade career, he became a prominent figures in the industry, and is among most influential actors of Indian cinema.
  • Nageswara Rao received seven state Nandi Awards, five Filmfare Awards South and a Tamil Nadu State Film Award.
  • He will Always Remain as "Balaraju" in telugu People Heart
  • NTR garu and ANR garu are said to be Two Eyes of Telugu Film Industry
  • Telugu Cinema Audience named him as "Natasamrat"

S. V. Ranga Rao

He was an acclaimed method actor, one of the first South Indian actors to win a Best Actor award at an international film festival for his role in Nartanasala (1963)

  • S.V. Ranga Rao's extensive filmography includes iconic roles in Telugu and Tamil cinema, with major films like Mayabazar, Pathala Bhairavi, Bhookailas, and Nartanasala.
  • He Played Historic Characters like as Ghatotkacha, Nepala Mantrikudu, Ravana, and Keechaka, respectively, earning him widespread acclaim for his versatile mythological and villainous portrayals. Other notable films include Bhakta Prahlada, Gundamma Katha, Devadasu, and Bala Bharatam.
  • He is known by the "Viswa Nata Chakravarthi"
  • Government of Andhra Pradesh instituted an award in his memory, presented every year to the best character acting performance, known as the S. V. Ranga Rao Award for Best Character Actor.

Savitri

Savitri is considered one of the finest actresses in Indian cinema history. Her performances still define acting excellence. Regarded as one of the greatest and most influential actresses, she was known as the "Queen of Telugu cinema".

  • Benchmark for emotional acting
  • Respected across all film industries
  • Savitri was a legendary Telugu actress, singer, director, and producer, often called the "first female superstar of South Indian cinema"
  • In a career spanning three decades, Savitri appeared in more than 250 films.
  • Savitri was among the highest-paid in South Indian cinema during the 1950s and 1960s and is often considered the "Queen of Telugu cinema".
  • She was popularly known by "Mahanati" which means the greatest Actress in Telugu Cinema.

K. Viswanath

Role: Director ¬∑ Cultural Filmmaker

Viswanath garu integrated classical arts into mainstream cinema, preserving Indian traditions through films. A prominent auteur, he received national and international recognition for blending classical arts with mainstream cinema in films like Sankarabharanam and Swathi Muthyam.

  • Revived classical music & dance
  • His films were powerful critiques of societal evils like the caste system (Saptapadi), dowry (Subhalekha), and untouchability, while also sensitively portraying physical and mental disabilities (Sarada, Sirivennela).
  • His film Swathi Muthyam was India's official entry for the Best Foreign Language Film at the Academy Awards, and Sankarabharanam also received international attention.
  • During the years, he has been honoured with 5 National Film Awards (for Sankarabharanam, Saptapadi, Swati Mutyam, Sutradharulu, and Swarabhishekam).
  • Honoured with 20 Nandi Awards (awarded by the Andhra Pradesh Government for films like Shruti Layalu, Subha Sankalpam and Kalisundam Raa etc.), 10 Filmfare Awards including Lifetime Achievement Award (for O Seeta Katha, Jeevana Jyoti, Saagara Sangamam, Subhalekha and others), and the prestigious Padma Shri Award.

S.P.Balasubrahmanyam

Commonly known as SPB or Balu, was an Indian playback singer, television presenter, actor, music composer, dubbing artist, and film producer.He was widely regarded as one of the greatest Indian singers of all time.He predominantly worked in Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, Malayalam, and Hindi films and sang in a total of 16 languages.

  • Balasubrahmanyam debuted as a playback singer on 15 December 1966 with the song "Emiyee Vinta Moham" for Sri Sri Sri Maryada Ramanna, a Telugu film scored by his mentor, S. P. Kodandapani.
  • Balasubrahmanyam gained international recognition with the 1980 film Sankarabharanam. The film is considered to be one of the best films ever to emerge from the Telugu film industry.Directed by K. Viswanath, the film's soundtrack was composed by K. V. Mahadevan and led to an increase in the usage of Carnatic music in Telugu cinema as well as in other languages.
  • Balasubrahmanyam received his first National Film Award for Best Male Playback Singer for his work. His first work in Hindi films was in the following year, in Ek Duuje Ke Liye (1981),for which he received another National Film Award for Best Male Playback Singer.
  • He was assigned as the default dubbing artist for Kamal Haasan in Telugu-dubbed versions of Tamil films. For the Telugu version of Dasavathaaram, he gave voice to seven characters (including the female character) out of ten characters played by Kamal Haasan.
  • He was awarded the Nandi Award for Best Male Dubbing Artist for the films Annamayya and Sri Sai Mahima.
  • Balasubrahmanyam hosted and judged Telugu musical reality TV show Padutha Theeyaga, which marked his television debut. Starting from 1996, the show is credited for unearthing singing talents from Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Singers like Usha, Kousalya, Gopika Poornima, Mallikarjun, Hemachandra, N. C. Karunya, Smita etc., have debuted in the show.
  • He has won six National Film Awards for Best Male Playback Singer. He was also awarded the Padma Shri in 2001 and the Padma Bhushan in 2011 and Padma Vibhushan in 2021

Konidela Chiranjeevi

He popularized genre films and achieved significant commercial success, becoming the highest-paid actor in India at one point and one of "the men who changed the face of the Indian Cinema".

Chiranjeevi revolutionized Telugu cinema by introducing new dance and stunt styles.

  • Dance & Action Revolution: He brought new energy to Telugu films by popularizing breakdancing and unprecedented stunt choreography, setting new benchmarks for action heroes.
  • Industry Hits & Box Office Dominance: He holds the record for eight industry hits, including Khaidi, Yamudiki Mogudu, Gang Leader, and Indra, solidifying his status as a mass entertainer.
  • Enduring Stardom: He has maintained his "Megastar" status for over four decades, continuing to deliver hits like Khaidi No. 150 and Sye Raa Narasimha Reddy.
  • Elevated Commercial Cinema: Films like Jagadeka Veerudu Athiloka Sundari blended fantasy with commercial appeal, while Shankar Dada MBBS showcased his versatility.
  • Elevated as "Megastar" for his dedication and hardwork.
  • CNN-IBN recognized him as one of "the men who changed the face of the Indian Cinema".
  • In 2024, he was honoured with a Guinness World Record as the most prolific actor-dancer in the Indian film industry.

Ram Gopal Varma

Ram Gopal Varma's impact on Telugu cinema is profound, primarily through his debut film Shiva (1989), which revolutionized the industry with its gritty realism, technical innovation (like the Steadicam), and new sound techniques, setting a new benchmark for action and gangster films.

  • Shiva (1989): A path-breaking college-based crime thriller that became an industry hit and redefined the action genre in Telugu cinema with its realistic violence and technical brilliance.
  • Kshana Kshanam (1991): A stylish and unconventional thriller that continued his new wave of filmmaking.
  • Redefined Action & Gangster Genres: Shiva and Gaayam changed the landscape of Telugu action and gangster films with their realistic, raw, and non-melodramatic style.
  • Introduction of Talent: Varma launched the careers of many actors and technicians, and several directors who assisted him went on to become successful filmmakers themselves (e.g., Puri Jagannadh, Krishna Vamsi).
  • Industry Benchmark: Shiva was a massive box-office success that set new benchmarks for what a Telugu film could achieve, inspiring confidence in ambitious projects.

S. S. Rajamouli

S.S. Rajamouli spearheaded the pan-Indian film movement by achieving massive, nationwide success with his Telugu films, particularly the Baahubali series, which broke language barriers through simultaneous dubbed releases

  • The Baahubali duology (2015, 2017) established the template for pan-Indian cinema, demonstrating the viability of a single film's narrative and spectacle appealing to audiences across different linguistic regions.
  • His films blend grand CGI with practical effects, epic action, and relatable human emotions, creating a universally appealing spectacle.
  • His subsequent film, RRR, further cemented this status, earning international accolades and solidifying the pan-Indian model's global recognition.
  • He expanded the market for Telugu and South Indian cinema into North India and international markets, previously dominated by Bollywood.
  • S. S. Rajamouli's duology of epic action films, Baahubali: The Beginning (2015) and Baahubali 2: The Conclusion (2017), became huge critical and commercial successes. Baahubali 2 became the highest grossing Indian film of all time.
  • RRR received various international accolades including an Academy Award and a Golden Globe Award for the song "Naatu Naatu", and a Critics' Choice Movie Award for Best Foreign Language Film.
  • He is also credited for expanding the market of Telugu cinema and South Indian cinema into North India and international markets.
